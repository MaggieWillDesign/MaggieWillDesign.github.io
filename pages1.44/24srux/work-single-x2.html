Momntm

Momntm is a fitness app featuring customized workouts and unwavering encouragement.

Project Overview


The Problem

Workout apps available in the market often fall short in providing users with personalized fitness options. Many of these apps follow a one-size-fits-all approach, neglecting the diverse range of fitness levels among their user base. As a result, these apps tend to become stagnant, leading to a decline in user engagement and interest due to the lack of customization.

The Goal

Give users a workout app that allows them to tailor workouts to their goals, ensuring steady strides toward a healthier lifestyle. Offer users a starting point that aligns with their individual fitness levels. Allow them to track their progress and witness their growth, providing the necessary motivation to sustain their fitness journey. Provide the convenience of custom workouts, accessible anytime and anywhere, helping users reclaim their time, improve their health, and enhance their well-being. Empower users with the motivation and confidence to embrace their fitness journey, regardless of their current position on the route to better health.

My Role: This is an individual design project that allowed me to plan and direct each step of the design thinking process as a solo Sr. UI/UX Designer.

Responsibilities: Conduct user research, define the problem, and provide insights to inform ideation. Define personas, user journeys, empathy maps, and user flows. Visual design of low-fi and high-fi wireframes, prototypes, and user testing. Brand design, voice, tone and marketing.

Duration: 6 Week Project - 2023

User Research Summary

To grasp user frustration, requirements, and preferences, I engaged in user research by conducting interviews and surveys. The aim was to acquire valuable insights into user needs and desires, enabling me to enhance the UX of the Momntum workout app. There are two types of user research methodologies: qualitative and quantitative research. I chose qualitative research collected through observations and conversations.

User Pain Points

1. Time Constraints

Many users, especially those with busy schedules or parenting responsibilities, the challenge of finding time for workouts. The limited time available can lead to difficulties in completing longer routines or sticking to a consistent exercise schedule. Several users mentioned that searching for workouts that fit within the time slot they have available can be a laborious task.

2. Lack of Customization

Users have expressed a prevailing sentiment that existing workout apps in the market adopt a one-size-fits-all approach, lacking the necessary level of customization required to align with individual goals for a healthier lifestyle. The consensus among users is that these apps often fall short in adapting to the diverse needs, preferences, and fitness levels of their user base.

3. Lack of Variety

Some users expressed a sense of monotony and boredom due to a perceived lack of variety in the available workouts. This can impact motivation, as users may become disengaged when faced with repetitive exercise routines.

4. Usability Challenges

Some users mentioned difficulties with the user interface of workout apps, such as confusing navigation or time-consuming searches for specific workouts. A user-unfriendly interface can hinder the overall experience and discourage users from utilizing the app to its full potential.

Usability Study

Parameters

Study Type:

Moderated usability study

Location:

Los Angeles, California

Participants:

5 participants

Length:

30-40 minutes

Findings

1. Finding

Users expressed the desire to manage all time-related features simultaneously, including intervals and rest periods. Additionally, they sought the ability to adjust the time before initiating the workout. Recognizing that time is consistently emphasized as a significant pain point, it is imperative to afford it a prominent position in both hierarchy and usability.

2. Finding

Users sought a method to save their settings or mark a favorite workout to streamline the customization process on subsequent occasions. Implementing this feature would not only save users time but also elevate the overall user experience, making the app more user-friendly.

3. Finding

While initial research indicated that users might benefit from specifying the workout environment, such as a small room versus a large room, usability studies revealed the contrary. The inclusion of this feature resulted in confusion and created a bottleneck in the flow. Removing this feature will enhance the overall experience and alleviate confusion.

Refine the Design

Styles & UI Kit, Mockups, High-fidelity Prototype & Accessibility

Refined Design: Styles & UI Kit Preview

Mockups

Informed by insights gained from usability studies, I tweaked the hierarchy and implemented design changes. This involved the removal of the environment feature and the consolidation of time-related settings. Furthermore, I introduced a favorites button, allowing users to swiftly apply their preferred presets and save time.

Refined Design: Before and After Usability Study

High-fidelity Prototype

Upon completing the low-fidelity wireframes, I proceeded to craft the final designs with a focus on simplicity and intuitiveness. I selected colors that convey a sense of fitness and athleticism, choosing red for emphasis. This choice is rooted in its frequent use in the sports community, and research in color psychology indicates that red promotes confidence among athletes. The headline font was selected for its attributes that evoke an energetic and dynamic ambiance commonly associated with sports. View Prototype

Accessibility Considerations

1.

I reviewed the product screens for WCAG AA Compliance. The initial design concepts included a greater amount of red in the background areas. However, I moved away from this approach due to concerns about color contrast, which presented accessibility issues. All of the new color combinations met the required standards, so I moved forward with the updated approach.

Going Forward

Takeaways, Next Steps & Feedback

Takeaways

Impact

With this project, I have gained valuable insights into the diverse approaches users have towards fitness and the recurring challenges they often face. While the app aims to cater to individuals of all fitness levels, there is significant room for further improvement and accessibility solutions. The initial goal was to initiate the process of providing more customization features, moving away from a one-size-fits-all approach.

What I learned

Throughout the design journey, I've gained valuable insights and expertise. Key lessons learned encompass:

  • Understanding user needs

  • Emphasizing the significance of hierarchy

  • Considering accessibility considerations

  • Effectively analyzing and synthesizing user feedback

Next Steps

1.

For an improved design, solicit input on user experience (UX) and user interface (UI) elements from colleagues specializing in the field. Actively involve peers with expertise in UX/UI design, urging them to offer constructive feedback on the existing design.

2.

Enhance the social aspects, fostering a sense of community where users can connect, share their experiences, and motivate each other on their fitness journeys. Building a supportive environment is integral to the vision.

3.

Focus on expanding accessibility features and brand voice, ensuring the app is usable and beneficial to individuals of varying abilities and needs.
