Partir

Partir is a location-based platform for people to easily share and borrow anything within their community with ease.

Project Overview


The Problem

Users often face the challenge of needing items for specific situations where purchasing may not be practical for infrequent use. Compact living spaces in urban areas and limited storage along with a growing interest in sustainability contribute to a desire for shared, sustainable solutions. Despite this need, no existing platform facilitates the efficient borrowing and sharing of items within communities.

The Goal

Create the sharing revolution and provide a platform that is a simple sharing hub where users effortlessly connect with others to share or borrow anything, all while promoting a sense of community and responsible consumption.

My Role: This is an individual design project that allowed me to plan and direct each step of the design thinking process as a solo product designer.

Responsibilities: Conduct user research, define the problem, and provide insights to inform ideation. Define personas, user journeys, empathy maps, and user flows. Visual design of low-fi and high-fi wireframes, prototypes, and user testing. Brand design, voice, tone and marketing.

Duration: 8 Week Project - 2023

User Research Summary

To understand user frustration, needs, and requirements, I conducted user research through interviews and user surveys for my project. My goal was to gain insights into the needs and wants of users so that I can better design the Partir app and responsive website. There are two types of user research methodologies: qualitative and quantitative research. I chose qualitative research collected through observations and conversations.

User Pain Points

1. Settling for Less

Users who are indecisive about purchasing an item may choose to forgo the expense and settle without it, often resulting in a subpar experience or unfavorable outcome.

2. Network Struggles

Many users face the frustration of investing considerable time and effort in the pursuit of individuals who are willing to share items.

3. Purchasing Dilemma

When considering a new purchase, users may need to budget or cut non-essential expenses. However, if the item isn't used regularly, it can contribute to clutter and occupy space.

4. Borrowing Freedom

Users seek a convenient solution for borrowing items without the long-term commitment and responsibility of ownership.

Accessibility Considerations

1.

I checked the product screens for WCAG AA Compliance. All color combinations passed except for the Lavender shade with the Highlighter text. I opted for a darker shade that passed compliance for the product UI color and kept the Lavender for the brand palette.

2.

Two typefaces are utilized DM Mono for headlines and Inter for body copy. Using monospaced and sans-serif font families ensures easy readability. A limited set of typefaces prevents the app from appearing fragmented and enhances user comprehension by providing clear focal points.

3.

I established a text hierarchy across the application, aiding users in discerning various sections and information displayed on the screen.

Going Forward

Takeaways, Next Steps & Feedback

Takeaways

Impact

I found the project gratifying because it addressed an issue that is relatable to my friends and me. I enjoyed bringing the design to life, while learning how to design an app that is helpful to its users. The UI and onboarding was my favorite when working on this project. I found this project particularly rewarding because it addressed a problem that resonated with both me and my friends. Creating the design and transforming it into a functional app was an enjoyable learning experience, aiming to provide value to its users. In conclusion, this project allowed me to delve deeper into the realm of design, expanding my proficiency in UX research and UI design.

What I learned

Throughout the design process, I've acquired valuable insights and expertise. Some of the essential lessons I've gleaned include:

  • Recognizing user requirements

  • Importance of hierarchy

  • Taking into account accessibility considerations

  • Analyzing and synthesizing user feedback effectively

  • Use of gestalt principles to organize

Next Steps

1.

To enhance the design, seek input on user experience (UX) and user interface (UI) aspects from colleagues who specialize in the field. Actively engage with peers who possess expertise in UX/UI design, encouraging them to provide constructive feedback on the current design.

2.

Explore additional avenues for connecting users who share common interests. For instance, individuals passionate about outdoor water activities should have an easier time finding and sharing items amongst themselves, fostering a sense of community.

3.

Iteratively improve the app, ensuring that it aligns more closely with user preferences and expectations. By actively responding to user input, the design evolves dynamically, fostering a continuous improvement cycle that contributes to an increasingly refined and user-centric application experience
