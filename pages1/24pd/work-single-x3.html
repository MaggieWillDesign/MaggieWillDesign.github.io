Flexfund

Flexfund is a cutting-edge retirement trading platform meticulously designed to cater to the unique needs of self-employed individuals.

Project Overview


The Problem

A growing number of young individuals are choosing self-employment, leveraging the myriad opportunities facilitated by online platforms and tools. The accessibility and versatility of these digital resources have empowered young people to explore diverse avenues for self-employment, ranging from freelancing and consulting to entrepreneurship. This shift reflects not only a desire for flexibility and autonomy but also a response to the evolving landscape of work in the digital age.

While the autonomy and flexibility of self-employment offer young individuals unprecedented control over their careers, there's a notable drawback – the potential loss of advantages associated with saving money for retirement through tax-deferred workplace accounts, such as a 401(k). The absence of employer-sponsored retirement plans can pose a challenge for those navigating the realm of self-employment.

The Goal

Recognizing this gap, there arises an opportunity for a dedicated platform to assist these users in optimizing their retirement savings. Such a platform could provide tailored solutions, offering insights into alternative retirement investment options, guiding them on tax-efficient strategies, and bridging the gap in retirement planning that self-employed individuals often face. By addressing these specific needs, this platform can become an invaluable resource for those navigating the unique financial landscape of self-employment.

The goal is a platform dedicated to offering tailored solutions for individuals navigating the intricate landscape of retirement savings. We go beyond mere investment options, providing a comprehensive array of financial tools and resources designed to empower users in achieving their retirement goals with confidence and clarity. By understanding the specific requirements and goals of the self-employed demographic, this platform strives to empower users with the flexibility, insights, and support necessary to optimize their financial strategies and build a robust path towards their long-term financial objectives.

User Research Summary

In order to gain a deeper understanding of user frustrations, needs, and preferences, we conducted a series of interviews and surveys. The primary goal was to gather valuable insights into what users require and desire, with the ultimate aim of improving the platform's user experience.

User Pain Points

1. Misoconceptions

Some young self-employed and freelance individuals harbor misconceptions and lack crucial awareness concerning retirement investing and tax benefits. Contrary to popular belief, retirement planning isn't solely reserved for those with traditional employment structures.

2. Risk and Volatility

Self-employed users are wary of market volatility and fear potential losses in traditional retirement investments. Some see cryptocurrency as an enticing alternative due to its perceived potential for quick gains, despite its own risks.

3. Learning Curve

Many self-employed individuals feel overwhelmed by the complexities of traditional retirement investment strategies and terminology. Understanding tax implications and the impact of economic downturns adds to their anxiety and uncertainty.

4. Lack of Control and Predictability

Self-employed individuals express frustration over the perceived lack of control in traditional retirement investments. Providing accessible education, simplifying investment processes, and offering tools to mitigate risks can help alleviate these challenges and foster greater confidence in retirement investing among this demographic.

Usability Study

Parameters

Study Type:

Moderated usability study

Location:

Los Angeles, California

Participants:

5 participants

Length:

30-40 minutes

Findings

1. Finding

Users aren't noticing the AI feature as much as we'd like, and they also seem unsure about what prompts to input. To address this, we need to emphasize the feature more and provide example prompts on each screen. Updating the AI feature to showcase suggested prompts on each screen will help users better understand the capabilities of the Flex AI feature.

2. Finding

Some users expressed concerns about the hierarchy of the sidebar navigation. They felt that the trade button was somewhat concealed and should be given more prominence. Additionally, it was suggested to incorporate the option to show and hide various account balances to achieve a cleaner UI.

3. Finding

Users exploring the trading functionality suggested that having a confirmation screen after submitting a trade would be more intuitive than immediately redirecting them to the orders screen. Incorporating a confirmation pop-up or an information screen detailing the action taken and providing guidance on next steps would enhance the overall user experience.

4. Finding

The watchlist tab was occasionally overlooked by some users, despite being a requested feature. To address this, we relocated the watchlist tab to the sidebar, ensuring it's easily accessible and consistent with other account views.

Refined Design: Welcome Screens

Accessibility Considerations

1.

I meticulously reviewed the product screens to ensure compliance with the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) Level AA standards. This involved a thorough examination of various elements such as color contrast, text size, keyboard navigation, and alternative text for images, among others.

2.

Jakarta Sans is the exclusive font family utilized across both platform design and marketing materials. Opting for sans-serif fonts prioritizes readability, ensuring effortless comprehension for users.

3.

I implemented a structured text hierarchy throughout the application, helping users to easily distinguish between different sections and information presented on the screen.

Going Forward

Takeaways, Next Steps & Feedback

Takeaways

Impact

The independent nature of self-employment often leads individuals to overlook the importance of retirement planning. Without the structure of employer-sponsored retirement programs or guidance from HR departments, many freelancers neglect this critical aspect of financial planning. However, adopting a proactive approach to retirement investing not only secures long-term financial stability but also empowers individuals to take control of their financial futures. The value of the platform lies in its ability to empower numerous users to seize retirement investment opportunities and leverage the benefits of compound interest and tax-deferred growth, thereby enabling them to build substantial retirement funds.

What I learned

In reflecting on my experiences with this case study, I've garnered valuable insights and expertise. Here are some key takeaways:

  • Recognizing the intricate design requirements for financial platforms

  • Understanding the potential impacts of AI on product efficacy, both beneficial and detrimental

  • The nuances of designing for dark mode

  • Prioritizing the user perspective to optimize outcomes

Next Steps

1.

For an improved design, solicit input on user experience (UX) and user interface (UI) elements from colleagues specializing in the field. Actively involve peers with expertise in UX/UI design, urging them to offer constructive feedback on the existing design.

2.

Enhance the social aspects, fostering a sense of community where users can connect, share their experiences, and motivate each other on their fitness journeys. Building a supportive environment is integral to the vision.

3.

Focus on expanding accessibility features and brand voice, ensuring the app is usable and beneficial to individuals of varying abilities and needs.
